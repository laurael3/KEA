Examples of colour themes in film

Mad Max: Fury Road

Directed by George Miller (2015)

Action Adventure Sci-Fi

In "Mad Max: Fury Road," color functions as a potent form of visual storytelling, conveying the brutality of the world and the stark juxtaposition between oppressive rule and the fight for freedom. The film's dynamic action sequences are expertly choreographed to amplify the impact of color, creating a visually captivating cinematic experience that immerses viewers in the vividly realized post-apocalyptic universe while enhancing the storytelling by highlighting the harshness of the dystopian landscape.

Edward Scissorhands

Directed by Tim Burton (1990)

Drama Fantasy Romance

In "Edward Scissorhands," director Tim Burton uses color to convey the stark contrast between the protagonist, and the suburban community he enters. The protaonist lives in a pale, almost ghostly world, symbolizing his isolation and otherness. In contrast, the colorful, cookie-cutter houses and residents of the neighborhood stand out in vivid pastels, emphasizing their conformity and superficiality. This visual opposite of the monochrome world and the vibrant, yet artificial, surroundings highlights the film's themes of the struggle to belong in a conformist society.

Joker

Directed by Todd Phillips (2019)

Crime Drama Thriller

In "Joker," color is used to show the transformation of Arthur into the Joker character. The film's early scenes are in muted colors, reflecting Arthur's bleak and downtrodden life. As his Joker persona progress, the color palette becomes more vibrant, signifying his liberation and madness. This deliberate shift in color shows his transformation from a marginalized, mentally ill individual who through a societal breakdown becomes a symbol of chaos and rebellion with the embrace of his Joker persona.
